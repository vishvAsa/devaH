nIlashikhaNDa

## Occurance
The term nIlashikhaNDa is an epithet of rudra,  
which, in the shruti, is only found in the  atharvaveda saMhitA-s.  
It is used thrice for rudra in the vulgate saMhitA  
and twice in the paippalAda saMhitA.  
The third occurrence in the vulgate has it as "nIlashikhaNDa-vAhanaH |"  
Depending on whether you take it as a tatpuruSha or a bahuvrIhi, mantra practitioners interpret it differently:  
as the vehicle of rudra  
or the one with the peacock-vehicle. 

Outside of the shruti, it is only found once in the mahAbhArata in the incantation recited by kR^iShNa and arjuna to invoke rudra  
when the latter had to reobtain the pAshupata missile to slay jayadratha.  
That incantation from the mahAbharata is rather notable  
because it contains several epithets of rudra found in the shruti  
and is likely intended as an invocation of nIlarudra,  
who is worshipped in the eponymous sUkta from the paippalAda saMhitA. 

Further, in this invocation kR^iShNa and arjuna use the phrase: 

> "namo brahma-vaktrAya |" 

This, in our opinion, is the earliest reference to pa~nchabrahma mantra-s outside of the shruti. 

After invoking rudra thus, kR^iShNa and arjuna obtain a vision of the god  
who instructs them to go to a lake and obtain the famed missile.  
There, they worship rudra again with the shatarudriya (the yajurvedic chant).  

Then nIlalohita rudra, emanated by mahAdeva, taught arjuna the secret of the missile. 


Some recensions of the mahAbharata append a variant of a section of the shatadrudriya in verse form to the incantation recited by kR^iShNa and arjuna:

> > नमो हिरण्यवर्णाय  
> हिरण्यकवचाय च ।  
कद्रुद्राय नमो विष्वक्-  
चक्षुषे च हनीयसे ॥  
मीढुष्टमाय हव्याय   
श्रविष्टाय श्रुताय च ।  
शिपिविष्टाय गिरये  
> नमो बुध्न्याय बिल्मिने ॥  
नमः पर्णाय गेह्याय  
> द्रुह्याय प्रभृशाय च ।  
नमः पर्णाय शुष्काय  
> रजस्याय पुलस्त्यये ॥  
दुंदुभ्याय हनन्याय  
> स्वायुधाय सुधन्वने ।  
नमः स्तुत्याय पथ्याय   
> काव्यायाशुरथाय च ॥  
नमः सोम्याय वात्याय  
> उर्वयाय निषङ्गिणे ।
वैशन्ताय च कूप्याय   
> नीपाय च वरूथिने ॥
नमः सोमाय रुद्राय  
> ताम्राय शिवमूर्तये ।
नमो ऽस्तु देवदेवाय  
> महाभूतधराय च ॥


In our opinion, these are footprints of an early shaiva tradition wherein the shatarudriya and the brahma-mantra-s played an important role.
