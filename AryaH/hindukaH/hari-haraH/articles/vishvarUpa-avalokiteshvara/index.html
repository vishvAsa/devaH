Source: TW

A curious mantra from the sarva-tathAgata-tattva-saMgraha, oldest of the surviving bauddha yoga-tantra in Sanskrit, to avalokiteshvara identifying him with harihara.

Consort

Vishwarupa, shown in the Hiranyavarna bauddha Vihara on gunla. This world bodied Vishnu with Rudrian and mahasankarshana features inspired the aniconic all devouring akaalpurakh of the Sikh religion.

No marks for guessing his consort. No consort in the image I think, but what I meant was seems like chandika ekanamsha vaishnavi becomes Narasimha patni Madhavi kali, kubjika Madhavi vidya of the Atharvanas, amurta kalasankarshini, siddhilakshmi as a consort to amurta Vishwarupa. The pancharatrika origins of the highest goddess of the system are thus remembered.

I also feel this transfunctional Madhavi of pancharatrika origins in the Kula movement was the primary inspiration for Vishwamata in the culminating Kalachakra system.(I wonder if the kamo yonih formula can be uniquely interpreted to suit this?)

Iconography

Could the bitherocephalic triple eyed Harihara conceived in Kashmir( iconographically close to neelakantha avalokiteshwara identified with harihara) be the original basis of the amurta parabrahman of the early Sikh religion?

Bingo..🥸‘Vaishnava advaitins’ invented a novel harihara iconography depicting Vishnu, tricephalic in kashmir or vitthala in deccan, with linga as his crown, instead of the ardhaswarupa.

World bodied Aniconism

Alright I think this Vishnuesque harihara would be Vishwarupa Vishnu essentially,whose taking to Rudrian motifs as vishwarupa led to an identification with harihara (and avalokit), which explains the aniconism and world bodied motifs in the schools inspired by this deity like sikhs.

Tried to hypothesize that Vishwarupa Vishnu conceived in Kashmir was identified with Harihara and Avalokiteshwara pan India, and eventually became the central god of medieval advaitin groups like warkaris and Sikhs with the world-bodied and aniconism motif.

but why did it became aniconic ?

In consolidation of the principle of Vishwarupa, he who has the world as his body.

This duo is, I think, the deity of the sarabhloh granth( which has an explicit maya lachhmi ji ustat, as a memory I think of the vaishnava origins of amurta ‘kalika’ and sarvloh Vishwarupa ‘akaal’, as Sri and Vishnu.

This unique harihara, whom bauddhas had already identified as neelakanthamurti avalokiteshwara , inspired evolutes of vaishnava advaitins like warkaris and nirakaravadin sikhs who in method were purely harinaamvadin-s, but the central god of their system was described with hariharatmaka motifs and very prominently as the all pervading purusha.

A bhagawatam oriented literature and mythology for a Harihara-esque deity is a mirror image of the iconography of this ‘Vishnu Harihara’ .

Further, The ramayatis who again endow the central god of their system with rudrian motifs and as the all pervading purusha unrestricted to idols(and all their aniconism in consolidation of this world bodied rama) are an evolute of this very group and the rama of these ramayati systems, a transmutation of this Vishnu Harihara. Which resolves the harihara-vaad of sagun sakar ramayati authors like Tulasidas and the prominence of ramnaam for nirakara parabrahman-vadin ramayatis like malook and kabir who dismiss the gunavataras and the dashavataras as mortal pashus subject to their transmutated harihara parabrahman ‘Ram’.

Karshni Udasis could be a minor krishnayati reflex of this phenomenon and their perception of gopala a parallel to the sikh and ramayati harihara coded parabrahman, but I am yet to see any material that confirms this.
