The gods are called upon to inspire the apprehension of this insight (often termed "causing it to flow"), leading to the composition of a mantra (e.g., the famous sAvitrI mantra of vishvAmitra gAthina). This concept was central to the early Indo-Iranian religion because it appears on the Iranic side as daenA, which eventually came to mean religion.

It is notable that, on the Iranic side, daenA is personified as a female deity, sometimes identified with the goddess ashi vanghuhi, or mentioned as her sister. We posit that such Indo-Iranian border-zone developments led to the emergence of a comparable goddess gAyatrI as the personification of the dhI mentioned in the sAvitrI. As we have noted before, on the Hindu side, a similar personification is also seen in the goddess AkUtI.

> गायत्रिया गायत्रीच्छन्दो विश्वामित्र ऋषिः सविता देवताऽग्निर्मुखं ब्रह्मा शिरो विष्णुर्हृदयँ रुद्रः शिखा पृथिवी योनिः प्राणापानव्यानोदानसमाना सप्राणा श्वेतवर्णा साङ्ख्यायनसगोत्रा  
गायत्री चतुर्विँशत्यक्षरा त्रिपदा॑ षटकु॒क्षिः॒ पञ्चशीर्षोपनयने विनियोगः 
> 
> इति तैत्तिरीयारण्यकान्ध्रपाठे।

This upaniShad is shared by the atharvan and KYV traditions. I hold that an older mantra to gAyatrI devI is in the AV saMhitA: AV-vulgate 19.71. It is used in the atharvan saMdhyA.
