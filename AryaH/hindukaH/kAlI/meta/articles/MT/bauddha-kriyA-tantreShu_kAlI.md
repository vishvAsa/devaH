kAlI in the bauddha kriyA tantra-s

The tAthAgata-s have a kAlI tantra known as the shrI-devI-kAlI-prashaMsA-tantra-rAja (Sanskrit original lost) of the kriyA tantra class in which vajrapANi, already foreshadowing his action in the capacity of a cosmic buddha, calls upon various deities and beings to compose stotra-s to kAlI. The whole tantra is a collection of these stotra-s. 

## vajrapANi, yama, demonization
In these, vajrapANi and other deities address her as the sister of yama. 

She retains the epithet relating to her bauddha demonization rendered in Tibetan translation as: "bdud kyi ma" = mother of the demon (mAra). Elsewhere in the bauddha corpus, she is named "bdud kyi ma" = wife of the demon (see below). 

## stotras
In all, there are 12 stotra-s suggesting an inheritance from the core 12 kAlI system of the early kAlI-kula. While the stotra-s have many bauddha elements woven into the Hindu background of the kAlI tradition, it shows signs of being specifically influenced by old kAlI-kula. 

The connection to kAma-kala is signaled by the epithet, the devI-rAj~nI of the kAma-loka –  a station conferred on her upon meeting the buddha. 

## kAla-durgA
While she is called the black durgA with the form of mahAdeva, and the liberation of rudra, she is also termed the "destroyer or rudra" to affiliate her with the bauddha tradition. 

## revatI
She is said to emanate the goddess rematI (=revatI the ancient goddess from the vaidika and kaumAra traditions) and is said to arise from a triangular yantra, with a yellow or red center. 

## samudrA
Additionally, she is described as emanating the marine goddess samudrA and numerous shakti-s with dark, untied hair and is also the mother of the DAkinI-s. 

## Lion
She is said to be surrounded by lions, tigers and wolves and is said to be accompanied by a lion. Additionally, her right earring is said to be of the form of a lion, while the left earring is said to bear the form of a snake. 

## Disease
Interestingly, like the epidemic goddess, she is said to ride a white-faced donkey. Signaling her connection to the epidemic goddess mArI, she is described as having a bag bearing the agents of diseases tied to her rear saddle straps.

## Composers
The following are the composers of the kAlI-stotra-s comprising the tantra:

1. shakra (indra)
2. vaishravaNa (kubera)
3. druma, the horse-headed king of the kinnara-s
4. pa~nchashikha, the lord of the gandharva-s
5. nanda, the king of the nAga-s
6. nava-mukha-nAga: the nine-headed king of the kumbhANDa-s
7. viprachitti, the king of the asura-s
8. vajrahiraNyAkSha, the lord of the birds
9. dyaus
10. yama (this stotra was popular among bauddha-s as a standalone scripture)
11. brAhmaNa vararuchi
12. R^iShi kauNDinya

## dhAraNI
Further, the bauddha scripture has a dhAraNI (devI-mahAkAlI-nAma-dhAraNI), which seems to have been derived from a larger tantra known as the the DAkiny-agni-jihvA-jvAlA-tantra (Sanskrit originals lost). It features a peculiar incantation to kAlI, whom it calls the sister of yama and the wife of a demon. This is evidently an implicit demonization of rudra. The mantra goes thus:

> OM ruru vitiShTha vadhotsi  
> ruru roru vitiShTha vadhotsi svAhA |

The mantra has some atypical or aberrant imperatives but is otherwise unremarkable and non-specific. ruru could mean roar from root ru (Class 2 = roar); vi-tiShTha = stand  (from root sthA); vadhotsi can be resolved as vadha (=slay)+ utsi (ut+ root: si=bind, class 5).

## shrI-devI-kAlI-nAmAShTa-shataka
The bauddha-s also have a text shrI-devI-kAlI-nAmAShTa-shataka – the 108 names of kAli – said to have been taught by the shAkya-siMha. There kAlI is termed a bodhisattva who is a rAkShasi. It is not entirely clear from the translation if the names count up to 108. Rather, the core of the text appears to be a stotra wherein the buddha praises kAlI in 14 verses, with the last "verse" being the samaya-mantra of kAlI:

> OM AH hUM shrIdevi-kAli-samayajA-DAkinI DAyA samayA svAhA ||

Thus, it identifies kAlI as a DAkinI and as the samayA goddess. This indicates a clear connection to the kaula tradition, where the central goddess of the kula is the samayA goddess.

## yoginI-s
Beyond the kriyA tantra-s, kAlikA is also one of the 8 yoginI-s invoked for abhichArika rites like stambhana and uchchATana in the mahAkAla-nAma-tantra-rAja of the anuttara-yoga-tantra collection: caNDeshvarI, carchikA, kAlikA, kulikeshvarI, khaNDaruhI, dantotkaTI, prachAlI, and mAheshvarI.

## Transmission
None of these texts gives the kAlI mantra-s found in the Mongolian spirit-calling ritual; nevertheless, they provide exemplars for the transmission of the kAli lore to Tibet, which could then have reached Mongolia.
