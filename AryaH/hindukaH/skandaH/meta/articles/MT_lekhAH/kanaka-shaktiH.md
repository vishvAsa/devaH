An inscription of the andhra monarch vAsiShThIputra shivashrI pulamAvI from approximately the mid-100s of CEs found at Sannati, Karnataka, indicates his kaumAra affliations:
bhagavaM mahAseno jayati bhagavaM kaNakasa(kti jayati) …  rAyA vAseTh)ip[u]to siva siri puLumAvi ANapayati |
Lord mahAsena is victorious, Lord kanakashakti is victorious … King vAsiTThIputta sivasiri-puLumAvi rules.
The name kanaka-shakti or kanaka-shakti-hastin (one with the golden spear) is rather unique in kaumAra tradition. It is found in the play mR^ichChakaTika of shUdraka, the brAhmaNa warrior who overthrew the andhra-s. There it is uttered in a mantra by sharvilaka, the brAhmaNa thief, before he breaks into his target's house. A similar mantra is also found in the archaic kaumAra tantra, the uttara tantra of the ShaNmukha-kalpa. In kaumAra tradition, the name kanaka-shakti was eventually replaced by hemashUla, which means the same, in the practice of the root-mantra "OM vachadbhuve namaH |"

* Another andhra monarch from a little later, vAsiTThIputta siri-khanda-sAtakaNNi (vAsiShThI-putra shrI skanda-shAtakarNi), has the name of the god in his personal name.
