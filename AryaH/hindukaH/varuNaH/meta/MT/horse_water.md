In the vedic tradition, the horse is connected to the waters and varuNa. This is a peculiar connection.  

Several modern equids dig wells with their hooves in dry periods. The equid-dug wells are very important to the ecosystem in several parts of the world. These include:


1. Horses;  
2. the donkey; 
3. At least some onager populations;  
4. Mountain and Grevy's zebras. 

Thus, it was an ancient behavior. Hence, it is conceivable that a part of the water connection with horses might have arisen from an early knowledge of this behavior when there were still wild/semi-feral horses on the steppe.

A curious reference to the horses of the marut-s brings this to mind:

> ashvAsa eShAm  
ubhaye yathA viduH 
pra parvatasya  
nabhanUMr achuchyavuH || 5.59.7c

> Their horses, as both [gods and mortals] 
know, have caused the waters of the mountain to emerge.


> Poseidon created the first horse and is the father of Pegasus. - kastUriH

Another point: while indeed there is a proto-IE connection between the waters and the horse it might have two distinct even dimensions that were merged. 

1. There is the fire in water associated with the horse; 
2. there is the spring as in Pegasus or a Neptune emergence of a new lake that might have connection to well-digging by equids.
